The final's strangest coincidence

Sunday's final pits Argentina's captain against a player he once held as a baby. Nineteen years on, that photograph looks less like chance and more like foreshadowing.

CAMP NOU, 2007
NEW YORK, JUL 2026
'07 → '26
In 2007, Lamine Yamal's parents won a charity photoshoot at Barcelona — the prize was a picture with a 20-year-old rising star named Lionel Messi, who cradled their five-month-old son in a bathtub in the away dressing room. Nineteen years later, that infant is Spain's teenage talisman, wearing the number 10 shirt Messi once wore, and standing between Argentina and a third World Cup.
"That photo is incredible. It's crazy that we're both playing in the World Cup now." — Messi
